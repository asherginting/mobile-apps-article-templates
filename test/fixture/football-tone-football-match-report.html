Hello and welcome

Newcastle: can they do it? Relegation certainties for almost the entire season, the Magpies now have a real chance of staying up – and a win today at Villa would return all the pressure on to Sunderland and Norwich.

This season has been one long horror for Toon fans: the team has looked doomed almost from the beginning. They’ve spent virtually all season in the relegation zone, suffered hammerings against Man City, Chelsea, Palace and numerous others – and Steve McClaren’s sacking seemed to come several months too late. Rafa Benitez, that Premier League firefighter par excellence, came in, but results failed to improve, and Newcastle looked to be on their way to the Championship after that 3-1 loss at Southampton in April.

And yet somehow, from somewhere, they’ve managed to find something. They’re now unbeaten in four – a run that includes excellent draws against Liverpool and Man City – and all of a sudden, things look promising. They’re above Sunderland and Norwich in 17th, albeit having played one game more, but they have that crucial element: momentum. Today’s a huge game: they could really do with three points.

And what of Villa? This grand old club from the West Midlands, as we all know, are already relegated, after a season that should give Villa fans nightmares for many years to come. After that 1-0 opening-day win at Bournemouth, it’s just been one long dose of abysmal. The feeble Remi Garde couldn’t rectify the mistakes of the blustering Tim Sherwood, and poor old Eric Black has seen the losing run continue: Villa have lost 11 straight league games. Eleven. Derby in 2007-08 were probably worse, but still: their performance this season has been utterly abject.

Surely Villa can’t keep this losing run going? Find out at 3pm local time, 10am ET, 3pm BST. Join us!
